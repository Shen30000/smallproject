Dr. Yuan Longping

The man who saved a billion lives

Dr. Yuan Longping, holding a bunch of rice - part of his life-long war on hunger.

Here's a timeline of Dr. Yuan Longping's life

  • 1930 - Born in Bejing, China
  • 1953 - Graduates from Southwest Agricultural College
  • 1960s - Gets the idea of hybridizing rice to increase its yield after reading of similar research that was underway successfully in maize and sorghum
  • 1970s - Uses a particularly important wild variety of rice within a breeding programme, which resulted in varieties with yields improved by 20 - 30%. For this achievement, Yuan Longping was dubbed the "Father of Hybrid Rice."
  • 2000 - Wins the State Preeminent Science and Technology Award of China
  • 2004 - Wins the Wolf Prize in Agriculture and the World Food Prize
  • 2014 - Mentions in an interview that genetically modified food would be the future direction of food and that he had been working on genetic modification of rice
  • 2021 - Died of multiple organ failure at Xiangya Hospital of Central South University
